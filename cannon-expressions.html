Cannon Expressions and Cannons

Cannon

Cannonical Expression is where a word is broken into her sound units. Sound units are called "Cannons". E.g. the Cannonical Expression of the word tomato is "to.ma.to". The Cannons are to, ma, to. "To", "ma and to" are the Cannons".

Seeing words as cannons and cannon expressions helps with reading, spelling, and pronunciation. The sound‑units guide the eye and ear. This is especially useful for difficult words or unfamiliar pronunciation.

For example, online source breaks the word contradiction as:

con.tra.dict.ion

In my system, the word breaks more naturally as:

con.tra.dic.tion

The cannons are con, tra, dic, and tion. These sound‑units give a clearer path for reading, spelling, and pronouncing the word.

When writing a word, the Cannons guide the spelling. When reading, the Cannons guide the pronunciation. When learning pronunciation, the Cannons make the sound‑units clear.

It takes a little practice, and I have not fully integrated this system into all my spelling, reading, and pronunciation but it already helps.

Example:

- re.duc.tion
- shi.pping
- on.to.lo.gy
- dis.tur.bance
- ex.pre.ssions
- pro.nun.ci.a.tions
