Cannon Expressions and Cannons

Cannon

A cannon is the smallest stable sound‑unit of a word. Cannons behave like pool balls: one cannon strikes the next, and the sequence of impacts creates the sound of the word. Words are built from chains of cannons whenever there is more than one sound‑unit.

Cannon Expression

A cannon expression is the full pattern of impacts inside a word. It shows each cannon in order, revealing how the sound of the word emerges from the chain of collisions.

Seeing words as cannons and cannon expressions helps with reading, spelling, and pronunciation. Instead of relying only on letters, the sound‑units guide the eye and ear. This is especially useful for difficult words or unfamiliar pronunciation.

For example, online source breaks the word contradiction as:

con.tra.dict.ion

But in my system, the word breaks more naturally as:

con.tra.dic.tion

The cannons are con, tra, dic, and tion. These sound‑units give a clearer path for reading, spelling, and pronouncing the word.


Once a word is broken into cannons, one can adjust the pronunciation at a personal level to make the word sound right. Knowing the cannons gives a quicker and more accurate sense of how the word should sound. If a particular cannon is difficult, one can practice that cannon alone.

When writing a word, the sound‑units (the cannons) guide the spelling. When reading, the cannons guide the pronunciation. When learning pronunciation, the cannons make the sound‑units clear and workable.

It takes a little practice, and I have not fully integrated this system into all my spelling, reading, and pronunciation — but it already helps.

Example:

- re‑duc‑tion
- shi‑pping
- on‑to‑lo‑gy
- is‑tur‑bance
